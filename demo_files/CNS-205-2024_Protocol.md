# PROTOCOL CNS-205-2024

**Title:** A Phase II, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study Evaluating the Safety, Tolerability, and Efficacy of CNS-205 Intravenous Infusion in Patients with Early-Stage Alzheimer's Disease

**Sponsor:** Florence Pharmaceuticals, Inc.
**Protocol Number:** CNS-205-2024
**Protocol Version:** 1.4 (Amendment 2)
**Original Protocol Date:** August 15, 2023
**Amendment Date:** January 10, 2024
**IND Number:** IND 145678
**ClinicalTrials.gov:** NCT05678901
**Phase:** Phase II
**Therapeutic Area:** CNS / Neuroscience — Alzheimer's Disease

---

## 1. Study Objectives

### 1.1 Primary Objective
To evaluate the change from baseline in the Clinical Dementia Rating-Sum of Boxes (CDR-SB) score at 18 months in patients treated with CNS-205 compared to placebo.

### 1.2 Secondary Objectives
1. To evaluate the change from baseline in the Alzheimer's Disease Assessment Scale-Cognitive Subscale 13-item (ADAS-Cog 13) at 18 months.
2. To evaluate the change from baseline in the Alzheimer's Disease Cooperative Study-Activities of Daily Living Inventory (ADCS-ADL) at 18 months.
3. To evaluate the change from baseline in the Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE) at 18 months.
4. To characterize the safety and tolerability profile of CNS-205, with specific focus on Amyloid-Related Imaging Abnormalities (ARIA), including ARIA-E (edema) and ARIA-H (hemorrhage).
5. To evaluate the change from baseline in brain amyloid burden as measured by amyloid PET SUVr at 18 months.

### 1.3 Exploratory Objectives
1. To evaluate changes in plasma p-tau181, p-tau217, and NfL as pharmacodynamic biomarkers.
2. To evaluate the relationship between APOE ε4 carrier status and clinical outcomes and ARIA incidence.
3. To evaluate changes in volumetric MRI brain measures (hippocampal volume, whole brain volume, ventricular volume) at 18 months.
4. To evaluate caregiver burden using the Zarit Burden Interview (ZBI) at 18 months.
5. To evaluate the pharmacokinetic profile of CNS-205 following repeated IV infusions.

---

## 2. Study Design

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter, parallel-group, dose-ranging study.

**Arms:**
- **Arm A:** CNS-205 10 mg/kg IV Q4W × 18 months
- **Arm B:** CNS-205 3 mg/kg IV Q4W × 18 months
- **Arm C:** Placebo IV Q4W × 18 months

**Randomization:** 1:1:1 (CNS-205 10 mg/kg : CNS-205 3 mg/kg : Placebo), stratified by:
- APOE ε4 carrier status (carrier vs. non-carrier)
- Clinical stage (MCI due to AD vs. mild AD dementia)
- Use of symptomatic AD medication at baseline (cholinesterase inhibitor/memantine: Yes vs. No)

**Sample Size:** Approximately 225 patients (75 per arm).

**Estimated Study Duration:** 30 months (12 months enrollment + 18 months treatment).

## 3. Eligibility Criteria

### 3.1 Inclusion Criteria
1. Age 50 to 85 years, inclusive, at the time of informed consent.
2. Clinical diagnosis of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) due to Alzheimer's disease or mild Alzheimer's dementia per NIA-AA 2018 research framework criteria.
3. MMSE score 20–28, inclusive, at screening.
4. CDR Global Score of 0.5 or 1.0 at screening.
5. Positive amyloid status confirmed by one of: (a) amyloid PET scan (visual read positive by central reader), or (b) CSF biomarkers (Aβ42/40 ratio below laboratory-established threshold).
6. Stable dose of cholinesterase inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine) and/or memantine for ≥ 3 months prior to screening, or not taking these medications for ≥ 3 months. No changes permitted during study.
7. Reliable study partner available who has frequent contact with the patient (≥ 10 hours/week), can accompany the patient to all clinic visits, and can provide independent assessment of the patient's condition.
8. Adequate vision and hearing for neuropsychological testing (corrective aids permitted).
9. Fluent in the language of the cognitive assessments at the site.
10. APOE genotyping performed (result not required for eligibility, but required for stratification).

### 3.2 Exclusion Criteria
1. Any medical or neurological condition (other than AD) that could contribute to cognitive impairment (e.g., vascular dementia, Lewy body dementia, frontotemporal dementia, Parkinson's disease, normal pressure hydrocephalus, brain tumor, HIV-associated dementia).
2. History of stroke or transient ischemic attack (TIA) within 12 months of screening.
3. MRI evidence at screening of: (a) > 4 cerebral microhemorrhages (microbleeds), (b) any area of superficial siderosis, (c) evidence of vasogenic edema, (d) prior macrohemorrhage > 1 cm, or (e) severe white matter hyperintensity burden (Fazekas score ≥ 3).
4. Current use of anticoagulants (warfarin, direct oral anticoagulants). Antiplatelet agents (aspirin ≤ 325 mg/day, clopidogrel) are permitted.
5. History of seizure disorder (except childhood febrile seizures).
6. Major psychiatric disorder (major depressive disorder with active suicidal ideation, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia) within 2 years. Stable, treated depression is permitted.
7. Clinically significant cardiovascular disease: NYHA Class III/IV heart failure, recent MI (< 6 months), unstable angina, uncontrolled hypertension (systolic > 160 or diastolic > 100 despite treatment).
8. Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C) or severe renal impairment (eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m²).
9. Known hypersensitivity to any component of CNS-205 or prior severe infusion reaction to any monoclonal antibody therapy.
10. Contraindication to MRI (e.g., pacemaker, ferromagnetic implants, severe claustrophobia unresponsive to anxiolytic premedication).
11. Contraindication to lumbar puncture (if CSF collection planned): bleeding disorder, anticoagulant use, infection at puncture site, increased intracranial pressure.
12. Participation in another interventional clinical trial within 60 days or 5 half-lives of the investigational agent (whichever is longer) prior to screening.

## 4. Schedule of Assessments

### 4.1 Screening (Day –45 to Day –1)
- Study partner consent and patient informed consent (or legally authorized representative)
- Demographics, medical/surgical history, AD history (age of symptom onset, prior AD treatments)
- Complete physical and neurological examination
- Vital signs (BP, HR, temperature, weight, height)
- 12-lead ECG
- Cognitive assessments: MMSE, CDR (with study partner interview), ADAS-Cog 13
- Functional assessment: ADCS-ADL (with study partner input)
- Neuropsychiatric Inventory (NPI-12)
- Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS)
- Laboratory assessments:
  - Hematology: CBC with differential
  - Chemistry: CMP (includes BUN, creatinine, eGFR, sodium, potassium, chloride, glucose, calcium, AST, ALT, albumin, total protein, total bilirubin, alkaline phosphatase)
  - Coagulation: PT/INR, aPTT
  - Thyroid function: TSH, free T4 (to exclude thyroid-related cognitive impairment)
  - Vitamin B12 and folate levels
  - RPR or VDRL (syphilis screening)
  - HIV 1/2 antibody
  - Hepatitis B (HBsAg), Hepatitis C (HCV antibody)
  - Serum pregnancy test (women of childbearing potential)
  - APOE genotyping (EDTA whole blood, central lab)
- Brain MRI: 3T (preferred) or 1.5T, volumetric sequences (3D T1 MPRAGE, T2 FLAIR, T2* GRE or SWI, DWI) per imaging charter. Central read for ARIA, microhemorrhage count, and volumetric measurements.
- Amyloid PET scan (florbetapir, florbetaben, or flutemetamol) with centralized visual and quantitative (SUVr) read — OR — lumbar puncture for CSF Aβ42, Aβ40, p-tau181, t-tau (if PET not available/preferred)
- Concomitant medication review
- Zarit Burden Interview (study partner)

### 4.2 Baseline / Day 1 (Randomization)
- Confirm eligibility
- Randomization via IVRS/IWRS
- Vital signs (pre-infusion, 15 min, 30 min, 1 hr, 2 hr post-start of infusion)
- Pre-dose blood samples: PK (4 mL serum), plasma biomarkers (p-tau181, p-tau217, NfL — 10 mL EDTA plasma)
- Administration of CNS-205 or Placebo IV over 60 minutes (first 3 infusions) → 30 minutes (subsequent, if tolerated)
- Post-infusion observation: **minimum 2 hours** for first 3 infusions, minimum 1 hour thereafter
- Adverse event assessment
- Concomitant medication review
- C-SSRS

### 4.3 Infusion Visits (Q4W: Months 1–18)
Visits at Weeks 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32, 36, 40, 44, 48, 52, 56, 60, 64, 68, 72 (18 infusions total).

**At every infusion visit:**
- Targeted physical examination
- Vital signs (pre-infusion, end of infusion, 1 hr post-infusion)
- Adverse event assessment (with specific ARIA symptom checklist: headache, confusion, visual disturbances, nausea, gait instability)
- Concomitant medication review
- C-SSRS
- Administration of CNS-205 or Placebo IV

**Additional procedures at specific visits:**

| Assessment | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Brain MRI (ARIA monitoring) | Weeks 4, 12, 24, 36, 48, 60, 72 (Q12W) plus unscheduled if ARIA symptoms |
| MMSE | Weeks 24, 48, 72 (Q24W) |
| CDR-SB (with study partner) | Weeks 24, 48, 72 (Q24W) |
| ADAS-Cog 13 | Weeks 24, 48, 72 (Q24W) |
| ADCS-ADL (with study partner) | Weeks 24, 48, 72 (Q24W) |
| NPI-12 | Weeks 24, 48, 72 |
| Blood biomarkers (p-tau, NfL) | Weeks 12, 24, 48, 72 (Q12–24W) |
| PK blood sample | Weeks 4, 12, 24, 48, 72 (pre-dose and 1 hr post-dose) |
| Laboratory (CBC, CMP) | Weeks 12, 24, 48, 72 (Q12–24W) |
| DLQI / Zarit Burden Interview | Weeks 24, 48, 72 |

### 4.4 End of Treatment (Week 72 / Month 18) — PRIMARY ENDPOINT
- Complete physical and neurological examination
- Vital signs
- 12-lead ECG
- Full cognitive battery: MMSE, CDR-SB, ADAS-Cog 13
- ADCS-ADL, NPI-12
- C-SSRS
- Laboratory assessments: CBC, CMP, coagulation, thyroid function
- Brain MRI (volumetric + ARIA monitoring)
- Amyloid PET scan (change from baseline)
- Blood biomarkers (p-tau181, p-tau217, NfL)
- PK blood sample (pre-dose, 1 hr, 4 hr, 24 hr post-dose for terminal PK)
- Zarit Burden Interview
- Concomitant medication review
- Adverse event assessment

### 4.5 Safety Follow-Up (Week 84 / 3 months post last dose)
- Targeted physical and neurological examination
- Vital signs
- Brain MRI (final ARIA monitoring)
- Adverse event assessment (focus on ARIA, infusion reactions)
- C-SSRS
- Concomitant medication review
- Blood biomarkers

## 5. Operational Requirements

### 5.1 Investigational Product
- **Storage:** 2–8°C (36–46°F), protected from light. Do not freeze or shake.
- **Preparation:** Dilute calculated dose in 250 mL 0.9% NaCl. Use within 4 hours of preparation.
- **Administration:** IV infusion over 60 minutes (first 3 infusions), then 30 minutes if tolerated. Dedicated infusion line with 0.2 μm filter.
- **Infusion site requirements:** Dedicated infusion chairs/beds, vital signs monitoring equipment, emergency resuscitation equipment, trained nursing staff for minimum 2-hour post-infusion observation (first 3 infusions).

### 5.2 ARIA Monitoring Protocol
- MRI performed Q12W for ARIA detection (ARIA-E: vasogenic edema; ARIA-H: microhemorrhage or superficial siderosis).
- **If ARIA detected:**
  - Mild/asymptomatic ARIA-E: Continue dosing at investigator discretion, repeat MRI in 4 weeks.
  - Moderate ARIA-E or symptomatic: Suspend dosing. Repeat MRI Q4W until resolution. May resume at reduced frequency upon resolution.
  - Severe ARIA-E or ARIA-H (new macrohemorrhage or > 2 new microhemorrhages): Permanently discontinue. MRI Q4W until stable.
- All ARIA events reported as AESIs (Adverse Events of Special Interest).

### 5.3 Cognitive Assessment Requirements
- All CDR and ADAS-Cog raters must be certified by central vendor before administering assessments.
- Rater consistency: Same rater should administer assessments to the same patient throughout the study.
- Study partner consistency: Same study partner should participate in CDR and ADCS-ADL interviews throughout the study.
- Blinded inter-rater reliability checks performed by central vendor Q6M.

### 5.4 Imaging Requirements
- **MRI:** 3T scanner preferred (1.5T acceptable if 3T unavailable). Specific sequences per imaging charter: 3D T1 MPRAGE (1mm isotropic), T2 FLAIR, T2* GRE or SWI (ARIA detection), DWI. Submit to central imaging lab within 48 hours via secure DICOM.
- **Amyloid PET:** Screening and Week 72 only. Florbetapir (18F-AV-45), florbetaben (18F-NAV4694), or flutemetamol (18F-GE067). Centralized quantitative analysis (SUVr with cerebellar reference region).
- **Lumbar puncture (optional alternative to PET at screening):** Performed by experienced operator, 12 mL CSF collected in polypropylene tubes, aliquoted within 1 hour, frozen at –80°C, shipped on dry ice to central biomarker lab.
