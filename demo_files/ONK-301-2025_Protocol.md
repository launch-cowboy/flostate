# PROTOCOL ONK-301-2025

**Title:** A Phase III, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study Evaluating the Efficacy and Safety of ONK-301 in Combination with Standard-of-Care Chemotherapy in Patients with Previously Untreated Locally Advanced or Metastatic Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC) Without Actionable Genomic Alterations

**Sponsor:** Florence Pharmaceuticals, Inc.
**Protocol Number:** ONK-301-2025
**Protocol Version:** 2.1 (Amendment 1)
**Original Protocol Date:** January 8, 2025
**Amendment Date:** March 15, 2025
**IND Number:** IND 156789
**EudraCT Number:** 2025-000123-45
**ClinicalTrials.gov:** NCT06789012
**Phase:** Phase III
**Therapeutic Area:** Oncology — Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

---

## 1. Study Objectives

### 1.1 Primary Objective
To evaluate the Overall Survival (OS) of patients treated with ONK-301 plus platinum-doublet chemotherapy compared to placebo plus platinum-doublet chemotherapy.

### 1.2 Secondary Objectives
1. To evaluate Progression-Free Survival (PFS) per RECIST 1.1 by blinded independent central review (BICR).
2. To evaluate Objective Response Rate (ORR) per RECIST 1.1 by BICR.
3. To evaluate Duration of Response (DoR) per RECIST 1.1 by BICR.
4. To evaluate Time to Response (TTR).
5. To characterize the safety and tolerability profile of ONK-301 in combination with chemotherapy.

### 1.3 Exploratory Objectives
1. To evaluate patient-reported outcomes (PROs) using EORTC QLQ-C30 and QLQ-LC13.
2. To evaluate the association between PD-L1 expression (TPS) and clinical outcomes.
3. To identify potential predictive biomarkers of response to ONK-301 using tumor tissue and circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA).
4. To characterize the pharmacokinetic (PK) profile of ONK-301 when administered with chemotherapy.
5. To evaluate the immunogenicity of ONK-301 (anti-drug antibodies, ADA).

---

## 2. Study Design

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter, two-arm study.

**Arms:**
- **Arm A (Experimental):** ONK-301 400 mg IV Q3W + carboplatin (AUC 5) + pemetrexed (500 mg/m²) Q3W × 4 cycles, followed by ONK-301 400 mg IV Q3W + pemetrexed 500 mg/m² Q3W maintenance.
- **Arm B (Control):** Placebo IV Q3W + carboplatin (AUC 5) + pemetrexed (500 mg/m²) Q3W × 4 cycles, followed by Placebo IV Q3W + pemetrexed 500 mg/m² Q3W maintenance.

**Randomization:** 2:1 (ONK-301 : Placebo), stratified by:
- PD-L1 TPS (< 1% vs. 1–49% vs. ≥ 50%)
- ECOG Performance Status (0 vs. 1)
- Number of prior lines of therapy (0 vs. 1)
- Geographic region (North America vs. Europe vs. Asia-Pacific vs. Rest of World)

**Sample Size:** Approximately 450 patients (300 ONK-301, 150 placebo).

**Estimated Study Duration:** 36 months (12 months enrollment + 24 months follow-up).

## 3. Eligibility Criteria

### 3.1 Inclusion Criteria
1. Provision of signed and dated informed consent prior to any study-specific procedures.
2. Age ≥ 18 years at the time of informed consent.
3. Histologically or cytologically confirmed Stage IIIB/IIIC (not amenable to curative surgery or radiation) or Stage IV non-squamous NSCLC.
4. No prior systemic therapy for locally advanced or metastatic NSCLC. Prior neoadjuvant/adjuvant chemotherapy is permitted if completed ≥ 12 months before enrollment.
5. Documented absence of actionable genomic alterations (EGFR, ALK, ROS1, BRAF V600E, NTRK, RET, MET exon 14). Testing must be performed by a CLIA-certified laboratory.
6. PD-L1 tumor proportion score (TPS) assessed by validated immunohistochemistry assay (22C3 pharmDx). Results must be available prior to randomization.
7. ECOG Performance Status of 0 or 1.
8. Measurable disease per RECIST 1.1 (at least one lesion ≥ 10 mm in longest diameter, or lymph node ≥ 15 mm in short axis, on CT/MRI within 28 days of enrollment).
9. Adequate hematologic function: ANC ≥ 1,500/μL, platelets ≥ 100,000/μL, hemoglobin ≥ 9.0 g/dL (without transfusion within 14 days).
10. Adequate hepatic function: total bilirubin ≤ 1.5 × ULN (≤ 3 × ULN for patients with Gilbert's syndrome), AST and ALT ≤ 2.5 × ULN (≤ 5 × ULN for patients with liver metastases).
11. Adequate renal function: creatinine clearance ≥ 45 mL/min by Cockcroft-Gault formula or 24-hour urine collection.
12. Adequate coagulation: INR ≤ 1.5 × ULN and aPTT ≤ 1.5 × ULN (unless on therapeutic anticoagulation).
13. Female patients of childbearing potential must have a negative serum pregnancy test within 72 hours prior to first dose and agree to use highly effective contraception during treatment and for 6 months after the last dose.
14. Male patients with female partners of childbearing potential must agree to use adequate contraception during treatment and for 3 months after the last dose.
15. Life expectancy ≥ 12 weeks.
16. Willing and able to comply with study visits and procedures.

### 3.2 Exclusion Criteria
1. Squamous cell histology (mixed tumors with any squamous component).
2. Prior therapy with any anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, anti-PD-L2, or anti-CTLA-4 antibody or any other antibody or drug specifically targeting T-cell co-stimulation or checkpoint pathways.
3. Active or prior autoimmune disease requiring systemic treatment within the past 2 years. Replacement therapy (e.g., thyroxine, insulin, physiologic corticosteroid replacement) is permitted.
4. Known active central nervous system (CNS) metastases. Patients with treated CNS metastases are eligible if clinically stable for ≥ 4 weeks, off systemic corticosteroids for ≥ 2 weeks, and without evidence of new or enlarging metastases on screening MRI.
5. Known history of or active interstitial lung disease (ILD) or pneumonitis requiring systemic corticosteroids.
6. Active infection requiring systemic therapy, including known active hepatitis B (HBsAg positive), hepatitis C (HCV RNA detectable), or HIV (HIV-1/2 antibody positive with detectable viral load).
7. History of allogenic organ transplantation or prior allogenic bone marrow transplant.
8. Receipt of live-attenuated vaccine within 30 days prior to first dose. Seasonal influenza vaccines for injection are permitted.
9. Major surgery within 4 weeks prior to first dose (minor procedures, e.g., port placement, allowed within 1 week if adequately healed).
10. Significant cardiovascular disease: NYHA Class III/IV heart failure, unstable angina, myocardial infarction within 6 months, clinically significant arrhythmia requiring medication, or QTcF > 480 ms on screening ECG.
11. History of myocarditis of any etiology.
12. Concurrent systemic corticosteroid therapy (> 10 mg prednisone equivalent/day) or immunosuppressive therapy. Inhaled/topical corticosteroids and adrenal replacement doses are permitted.
13. Pregnant or breastfeeding women.
14. Known hypersensitivity to any component of ONK-301, carboplatin, or pemetrexed.
15. Any condition that, in the opinion of the investigator, would interfere with evaluation of the study treatment or patient safety.

## 4. Schedule of Assessments

### 4.1 Screening (Day –28 to Day –1)
- Informed consent
- Demographics, medical/surgical history, smoking history
- Complete physical examination
- ECOG Performance Status
- Vital signs (weight, height, BP, HR, temperature, respiratory rate, SpO2)
- 12-lead ECG (performed in triplicate, at least 1 minute apart)
- Laboratory assessments:
  - Hematology: CBC with differential (WBC, ANC, lymphocytes, platelets, hemoglobin, hematocrit)
  - Chemistry: BUN, creatinine, sodium, potassium, chloride, bicarbonate, glucose, calcium, magnesium, phosphorus, total protein, albumin
  - Hepatic panel: AST, ALT, alkaline phosphatase, total and direct bilirubin, GGT, LDH
  - Coagulation: PT/INR, aPTT
  - Thyroid function: TSH, free T4
  - Urinalysis with microscopy
  - Serum pregnancy test (women of childbearing potential)
- Glomerular filtration rate (CrCl by Cockcroft-Gault)
- Tumor imaging: CT chest/abdomen/pelvis with contrast, brain MRI with gadolinium
- Tumor tissue collection for PD-L1 testing (if not already performed) and biomarker analysis (archival or fresh biopsy)
- ctDNA blood sample (10 mL whole blood in Streck cfDNA BCT tube)
- Concomitant medication review
- Adverse event assessment (baseline)
- EORTC QLQ-C30 and QLQ-LC13 questionnaires

### 4.2 Cycle 1 Day 1 (C1D1 — Baseline/Randomization)
- Confirm eligibility, randomization
- Targeted physical examination
- ECOG Performance Status
- Vital signs (pre-dose, 30 min post-dose, 1 hr post-dose)
- Pre-dose laboratory assessments: hematology, chemistry, hepatic panel, thyroid function
- Pre-dose PK blood sample (4 mL serum, shipped frozen on dry ice)
- Anti-drug antibody (ADA) blood sample
- Administration of ONK-301/Placebo IV over 30 minutes
- Administration of pemetrexed IV over 10 minutes
- Administration of carboplatin IV over 15-60 minutes
- Post-dose PK samples: 1 hr (±15 min), 2 hr (±15 min), 4 hr (±30 min)
- Post-infusion observation: minimum 60 minutes for first infusion
- Adverse event assessment
- Concomitant medication review
- Dispense patient diary

### 4.3 Cycle 1 Day 8 (C1D8)
- Targeted physical examination
- Vital signs
- Laboratory assessments: hematology (CBC w/diff)
- Adverse event assessment
- Concomitant medication review

### 4.4 Cycle 1 Day 15 (C1D15)
- Targeted physical examination
- Vital signs
- Laboratory assessments: hematology (CBC w/diff), chemistry, hepatic panel
- Adverse event assessment
- Concomitant medication review

### 4.5 Cycles 2–4 Day 1 (Q3W Treatment Phase)
- Targeted physical examination
- ECOG Performance Status
- Vital signs (pre-dose, 30 min post-dose)
- Pre-dose laboratory assessments: hematology, chemistry, hepatic panel, thyroid function (Cycles 2, 4)
- Pre-dose PK blood sample (Cycle 3 Day 1 only)
- ADA blood sample (Cycle 3 Day 1 only)
- Administration of ONK-301/Placebo IV over 30 minutes
- Administration of pemetrexed IV over 10 minutes
- Administration of carboplatin IV over 15-60 minutes (Cycles 2-4 only)
- Post-infusion observation: minimum 30 minutes
- Adverse event assessment
- Concomitant medication review
- EORTC QLQ-C30 and QLQ-LC13 (Cycle 3 Day 1)

### 4.6 Tumor Response Assessment (Q6W ± 7 days)
- CT chest/abdomen/pelvis with contrast (Weeks 6, 12, 18, 24, then Q12W thereafter)
- Brain MRI with gadolinium (if baseline brain metastases present: Weeks 6, 12, 18, 24, then Q12W)
- Response assessment per RECIST 1.1 by investigator and submitted for blinded independent central review (BICR)
- ctDNA blood sample (at each imaging assessment)

### 4.7 Maintenance Phase (Q3W until progression or intolerable toxicity)
- Targeted physical examination
- ECOG Performance Status
- Vital signs (pre-dose, 30 min post-dose)
- Pre-dose laboratory assessments: hematology, chemistry, hepatic panel, thyroid function (Q6W)
- Administration of ONK-301/Placebo IV over 30 minutes
- Administration of pemetrexed IV over 10 minutes
- Post-infusion observation: minimum 30 minutes
- Adverse event assessment
- Concomitant medication review
- EORTC QLQ-C30 and QLQ-LC13 (Q12W)
- PK sample (Q12W, pre-dose only)

### 4.8 End of Treatment (EOT) Visit (within 30 days of last dose)
- Complete physical examination
- ECOG Performance Status
- Vital signs
- 12-lead ECG (triplicate)
- Laboratory assessments: hematology, chemistry, hepatic panel, coagulation, thyroid function, urinalysis
- Tumor imaging (if not performed within prior 6 weeks): CT chest/abdomen/pelvis, brain MRI
- ctDNA blood sample
- ADA blood sample
- Adverse event assessment
- Concomitant medication review
- EORTC QLQ-C30 and QLQ-LC13
- Survival status

### 4.9 Safety Follow-Up (90 days after last dose)
- Telephone or in-person visit
- Adverse event assessment (focus on immune-related AEs and treatment-related SAEs)
- Concomitant medication review (for AE management)
- Survival status

### 4.10 Survival Follow-Up (Q12W after safety follow-up)
- Telephone or in-person contact
- Survival status
- Subsequent anticancer therapy received
- Continue until death, withdrawal of consent, lost to follow-up, or study completion

## 5. Operational Requirements

### 5.1 Investigational Product
- **Storage:** 2–8°C (36–46°F), protected from light. Do not freeze.
- **Preparation:** Dilute in 250 mL 0.9% NaCl. Prepared solution stable for 24 hours at 2–8°C or 6 hours at room temperature.
- **Administration:** IV infusion over 30 minutes via dedicated line with 0.2 μm in-line filter.
- **Drug accountability:** Real-time IVRS/IWRS tracking. All vials must be reconciled at each monitoring visit per TMF Zone 06.01.05.

### 5.2 Companion Diagnostic
- PD-L1 testing using PD-L1 IHC 22C3 pharmDx assay is **required** for stratification prior to randomization. Testing must be performed at a Dako-certified laboratory.

### 5.3 Biomarker Samples
- Tumor tissue (archival or fresh biopsy): 15 unstained FFPE slides or tissue block
- ctDNA: 10 mL whole blood in Streck cfDNA BCT tubes. Ship at ambient temperature within 72 hours to central lab.
- PK samples: 4 mL serum in red-top vacutainer. Process within 60 minutes. Ship frozen on dry ice.
- ADA samples: 4 mL serum. Process within 60 minutes. Ship frozen on dry ice.

### 5.4 Imaging Requirements
- All tumor imaging submitted to central imaging core lab (BICR) within 48 hours via secure DICOM upload.
- CT: ≤ 5 mm slice thickness, IV contrast required (unless contraindicated).
- Brain MRI: 1.5T or 3T, with gadolinium, T1, T2, FLAIR, DWI sequences per imaging charter.
