# PROTOCOL DERM-110-2025

**Title:** A Phase III, Randomized, Double-Blind, Vehicle-Controlled Study Evaluating the Efficacy and Safety of Topical DERM-110 Cream 0.5% in Adult Patients with Moderate to Severe Chronic Plaque Psoriasis

**Sponsor:** Florence Pharmaceuticals, Inc.
**Protocol Number:** DERM-110-2025
**Protocol Version:** 1.0
**Protocol Date:** May 20, 2025
**IND Number:** IND 167890
**ClinicalTrials.gov:** NCT06890123
**Phase:** Phase III
**Therapeutic Area:** Dermatology — Plaque Psoriasis

---

## 1. Study Objectives

### 1.1 Primary Objective
To determine the proportion of patients achieving a Psoriasis Area and Severity Index 75% improvement (PASI 75) response at Week 16.

### 1.2 Secondary Objectives
1. To evaluate the proportion of patients achieving an Investigator's Global Assessment (IGA) score of 0 (clear) or 1 (almost clear) with ≥ 2-grade improvement from baseline at Week 16.
2. To evaluate the change from baseline in Dermatology Life Quality Index (DLQI) at Week 16.
3. To evaluate the proportion of patients achieving PASI 90 at Week 16.
4. To evaluate the time to onset of PASI 50 response.
5. To evaluate the safety and tolerability of DERM-110 Cream 0.5%.

### 1.3 Exploratory Objectives
1. To evaluate the proportion of patients maintaining PASI 75 response at Week 20 (4 weeks after treatment cessation).
2. To evaluate the change in pruritus severity using a Numeric Rating Scale (NRS 0–10) at Weeks 4, 8, 12, and 16.
3. To evaluate biomarkers of local skin inflammation (IL-17A, IL-23, TNF-α) from optional skin biopsies.

---

## 2. Study Design

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, vehicle-controlled, multicenter, parallel-group study.

**Arms:**
- **Arm A (Experimental):** DERM-110 Cream 0.5%, applied topically twice daily (BID) to all affected areas for 16 weeks.
- **Arm B (Control):** Vehicle cream (identical in appearance), applied topically BID to all affected areas for 16 weeks.

**Randomization:** 1:1 (DERM-110 : Vehicle), stratified by:
- Baseline PASI score (12–20 vs. > 20)
- Prior biologic therapy (Yes vs. No)
- Geographic region (North America vs. Europe)

**Sample Size:** Approximately 300 patients (150 per arm).

**Estimated Study Duration:** 28 weeks (Screening through Week 20 follow-up).

---

## 3. Eligibility Criteria

### 3.1 Inclusion Criteria
1. Age ≥ 18 years at the time of informed consent.
2. Clinical diagnosis of chronic plaque psoriasis for at least 6 months prior to screening.
3. PASI score ≥ 12 at both Screening and Baseline visits.
4. Affected body surface area (BSA) ≥ 10% at both Screening and Baseline visits.
5. IGA score ≥ 3 (moderate) at both Screening and Baseline visits.
6. Candidate for topical therapy (not requiring systemic or biologic therapy at enrollment per investigator judgment).
7. Stable psoriasis (no significant flares or improvement within 4 weeks prior to screening per investigator assessment).
8. Willing to avoid excessive sun exposure and use of tanning beds during the study.

### 3.2 Exclusion Criteria
1. Non-plaque forms of psoriasis (guttate, inverse, pustular, or erythrodermic psoriasis) as sole or predominant form.
2. Drug-induced psoriasis (e.g., new onset or exacerbation from beta-blockers, lithium, antimalarials).
3. Prior use of any targeted biologic therapy (anti-TNF, anti-IL-17, anti-IL-23, anti-IL-12/23) within 3 months or 5 half-lives (whichever is longer) prior to Baseline.
4. Use of systemic psoriasis therapies (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, apremilast) within 4 weeks prior to Baseline.
5. Use of topical psoriasis therapies (corticosteroids, vitamin D analogues, calcineurin inhibitors, tar) within 2 weeks prior to Baseline. Emollients without active ingredients are permitted.
6. Use of phototherapy (UVB, PUVA) within 4 weeks prior to Baseline.
7. Active skin infection at any treatment site requiring systemic antibiotics, antivirals, or antifungals.

## 4. Schedule of Assessments

### 4.1 Screening (Day –14 to Day –1)
- Informed consent
- Demographics, medical/surgical history, psoriasis history (age of onset, prior therapies, disease course)
- Dermatology examination: PASI scoring, BSA calculation (Rule of Nines), IGA assessment
- Complete physical examination
- Vital signs (BP, HR, temperature, weight)
- Laboratory assessments:
  - Hematology: CBC with differential
  - Chemistry: BMP (sodium, potassium, chloride, BUN, creatinine, glucose, calcium, bicarbonate)
  - Hepatic panel: AST, ALT, total bilirubin, alkaline phosphatase
  - Pregnancy test (serum, women of childbearing potential)
- Concomitant medication review (including washout verification)
- Medical photography: standardized 2D photography of up to 3 target lesions using calibrated dermatology camera (see Imaging Manual)
- DLQI questionnaire
- Pruritus NRS (0–10)

### 4.2 Baseline / Day 1 (Randomization)
- Confirm eligibility (re-assess PASI, BSA, IGA)
- Randomization via IVRS/IWRS
- Vital signs
- Dispense study drug (4-week supply) with application diary
- First application training: demonstrate proper application technique, coverage, and drying time
- Adverse event assessment (baseline)
- DLQI questionnaire
- Pruritus NRS
- Optional skin biopsy (4 mm punch, from target lesion and non-lesional skin, 2 samples total)

### 4.3 Week 2 (Day 15 ± 3 days) — Telephone Visit
- Adverse event assessment (telephone)
- Concomitant medication review
- Compliance assessment (diary review)
- Address application technique questions

### 4.4 Week 4 (Day 29 ± 3 days)
- Dermatology examination: PASI, BSA, IGA
- Vital signs
- Adverse event assessment
- Concomitant medication review
- Medical photography (target lesions)
- Pruritus NRS
- Drug compliance assessment (tube weight, diary); dispense 4-week supply
- Urine pregnancy test (women of childbearing potential)

### 4.5 Week 8 (Day 57 ± 3 days)
- Dermatology examination: PASI, BSA, IGA
- Vital signs
- Laboratory assessments: hematology, chemistry, hepatic panel
- Adverse event assessment
- Concomitant medication review
- Medical photography (target lesions)
- Pruritus NRS
- DLQI questionnaire
- Drug compliance assessment; dispense 4-week supply

### 4.6 Week 12 (Day 85 ± 3 days)
- Dermatology examination: PASI, BSA, IGA
- Vital signs
- Adverse event assessment
- Concomitant medication review
- Medical photography (target lesions)
- Pruritus NRS
- Drug compliance assessment; dispense 4-week supply
- Urine pregnancy test

### 4.7 Week 16 (Day 113 ± 3 days) — PRIMARY ENDPOINT
- Dermatology examination: PASI (primary endpoint assessment), BSA, IGA
- Complete physical examination
- Vital signs
- Laboratory assessments: hematology, chemistry, hepatic panel
- Adverse event assessment

## 5. Operational Requirements

### 5.1 Study Drug
- **Storage:** Room temperature (15–30°C / 59–86°F). Do not refrigerate or freeze.
- **Packaging:** Identical laminate tubes (60 g each), blinded labeling with unique kit number.
- **Application:** Thin layer to all affected areas BID (morning and evening), at least 8 hours apart. Allow to dry before dressing.
- **Accountability:** Tube weights recorded at dispensing and return at Weeks 4, 8, 12, 16 per TMF Zone 06.01.05.

### 5.2 Photography Requirements
- Standardized 2D photography using calibrated dermatology camera system at every in-clinic visit.
- Minimum 3 target lesions documented with consistent positioning, lighting, and ruler for scale.
- Central blinded reading of all images by dermatology imaging core lab.
- Images uploaded to secure portal within 72 hours of capture.

### 5.3 ePRO Requirements
- Patients trained on eDiary application at Baseline for daily pruritus NRS reporting.
- Compliance alert: site contacted if patient misses > 3 consecutive daily entries.
